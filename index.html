PI
Professor Nancy Aggarwal

Professor Nancy Aggarwal did her undergraduate studies at IIT Bombay majoring in Engineering Physics with honors in Physics and a minor in Electrical Engineering. She then went to MIT to conduct research on the LIGO detector for her PhD. Her focus was to improve the quantum measurement uncertainty in gravitational wave detectors using specially engineered quantum states of light, for example squeezed states. After her PhD, she joined Northwestern University as a joint postdoctoral fellow at Center for Fundamental Physics and Center for Interdisciplinary and Exploratory Research in Astrophysics. She started as an assistant professor at UC Davis in 2023. Aggarwalâ€™s interests lie in using precision measurements to probe topics in fundamental physics, for example gravitational waves, dark matter, and studies of the quantum nature of gravity. She is a member of the LIGO Scientific Collaboration, LSD Collaboration, and ARIADNE Collaboration. She is also a co-founder of an international initiative called Ultra-High Frequency Gravitational Wave Initiative which brings together community members interested in ultra-high-frequency GW detection through regular workshops and review articles.
